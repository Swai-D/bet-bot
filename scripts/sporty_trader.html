How do you know if a football prediction is reliable?

Before listing the five essential tips for successful football predictions, the first step is to assess the reliability of your prediction. To do this, it's quite simple: you need to compare the odds offered by the bookmakers for your prediction with the likelihood of it coming to fruition.

In concrete terms, let's assume that you want to validate your Premier League prediction and bet on the match between Manchester City and Tottenham, for which Manchester City is the favourite. In this case, the most reliable prediction will be a victory for Man City, as the defending champions have more quality than Tottenham. The chances of a Citizens’ victory are therefore greater than those of Spurs.

In this particular context, if the odds offered by sports betting sites are 1.50 for a Tottenham win (and therefore a Man City defeat), the "Tottenham to win" prediction will not be considered reliable. Don't forget to analyse the probabilities before placing your football prediction. To do this, here is a list of free tips to consider before placing your bet.

Tip 2: Study team motivation

Now that the bookmakers' context is set, the second thing you need to know is that motivation will systematically influence a football club's performance on the pitch. The stakes involved in a match (title, Champions League qualification, relegation to lower division, etc.) or the size of the match bonus are key factors in motivating a football team, for example.

But team performance can also be influenced by other factors. Is a player in a contract dispute? A new recruit is announced? Is an important player having family problems? These factors can also have a major influence on team and player motivation. When you consider that the difference in motivation between two teams can often make the difference in a football match, it's easy to see why this criterion is so important to the success of a prediction. At the end of the season, for example, it's not uncommon to see a big Premier League club with nothing left to play for being held to a draw by a club looking to avoid relegation into the Championship. In fact, it's quite common.

In modern football, the combination of sufficient motivation and a positive frame of mind is often decisive. Take the domestic cups, for example: every year, the over-motivated and happy "little thumbs" upset the odds against Premier League or Championship teams. The same phenomenon can be observed at the World Cup and Euros, where countries considered less strong put in impressive performances in the final stages. The general state of mind of the team can be seen in the statements made by the players, the coach or the directors of a football club.

Announcements such as the appointment of a new coach can also have an effect on the team's short-term performance (often referred to as the “new manager bounce”). A change of coach desired by the players will tend to bind the team together and improve its performance in the matches following his appointment. The opposite is true of a coach who has been abandoned by the dressing room, but kept in place by the sporting directors. So take that into account.

Tip 3: Read the news to find out how the teams are faring

As well as analysing the teams' motivation, don't hesitate to keep abreast of the latest football news before placing your bet. It's always best to bet at the last minute, or at least until the lineups are announced (usually one hour before kickoff). Injuries, suspensions and other illnesses suffered by first-team players can have a big impact on the final outcome of a match.

Don't forget, moreover, that they will have an even greater influence on a team's results if it has a limited squad. So take a close look at the team's results with or without the absent player, and you'll have a very good idea of what to predict for a match. Your sports predictions will be all the better for it.

It's also important to take into account the form of the team as a whole. You need to differentiate between home form, away form and overall form. Be careful! A series of wins over very weak teams is not necessarily a sign of resplendent form. If Real Madrid have just beaten Rayo Vallecano 6-0, but before that had suffered three defeats against Barça, Atlético and Real Sociedad, their balance will remain fragile. A key factor to keep in mind.

Tip 4: Analyse the particular skills of the teams

The fourth and penultimate major tip from our football specialists is to take into account the specific characteristics of each team in your predictions before placing your bets. Some teams are traditionally strong at home, while others have a reputation for travelling well. Teams with smaller squads often struggle in the middle of the league, particularly when they are involved in the Conference League, Europa League or Champions League.

The history of previous encounters also gives an idea of a team's ability to take on another team or another style of play. While this criterion is more important in individual sports, where an opponent's style of play may be more or less suited to a player, it should not be overlooked in team sports. History tends to repeat itself, and some teams are particularly successful against others.

So, even though the squads may have changed completely since the last duel, the special atmosphere surrounding certain matches remains the same year after year and often influences the result. This is the case, for example, with the famous derbies, rivalries between two teams, (Arsenal-Tottenham, Manchester United-Manchester City, Milan-Inter, Atlético-Real Madrid, Lille-Lens...). Some teams will tend to galvanise in such situations, while others will collapse completely. Something to bear in mind when making football betting predictions.

Tip 5: Determine whether the odds offered are attractive

Last but not least, you should also assess whether the odds offered by the bookmakers are really worthwhile for your football prediction. As mentioned earlier on this page, it's vital to compare the odds offered by the bookmakers with the probabilities you yourself give the various outcomes after an in-depth analysis of the match in question. You can do this using the following formula: 1/odds x 100 = probability of an event occurring according to the bookmakers.

Let's take the example of a match between Arsenal and Tottenham, on which Bet365 offers odds of 2.20 for the Gunners to win and 2.80 for a Spurs victory, to illustrate:

  • 1 /2.20 x 100 = 45
  • 1 / 2.80 x 100 = 36

According to Bet365, Arsenal have a 45% chance of winning the match, while Spurs have a 36% chance. If you think, for example, that Tottenham have a much better chance of winning given the context, then you’ve identified a bet with a good amount of value and should therefore bet on the Lilywhites. This, of course, also rings true for the opposite scenario for an Arsenal win, as well as even betting on the draw. This is a calculation that should be made systematically before placing your bet, especially for a football prediction that will be subject to a large stake.
